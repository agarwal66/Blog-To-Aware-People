Awareness of Air Quality

Sources of Pollution

It's important to be aware of the sources of pollution in your area. This could include things like traffic, industrial emissions, and natural sources like wildfires or dust storms.hat is the main reason why so many countries struggle with this problem regardless of increasing accessibility to green energy and other solutions that can provide us with the cleaner air.

Health Effects

Poor air quality can have a range of health effects, including respiratory problems, heart disease, and even cancer. It's important to be aware of these potential health risks so you can take steps to protect yourself.The main pathway of exposure from air pollution is through the respiratory tract

Air Quality Index

The air quality index (AQI) is a measure of how polluted the air is in a specific area. It takes into account factors like particulate matter, ozone, and other pollutants. You can check your local AQI to see if it's safe to be outside or if you need to take precautions like wearing a mask.

Ways to Protect Yourself

There are several things you can do protect yourself from poor air quality, such staying indoors on days when AQI is high, wearing a mask when you're outside, and avoiding exercising in areas with high levels of pollution.

Climate Change

Climate change can have a significant impact on air quality, as rising temperatures can lead to more wildfires and increased levels of ozone. It's important to be aware of the connection between climate change and air quality so we can take steps to address both issues.
